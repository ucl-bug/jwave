    jwave.acoustics.operators

    helmholtz

    Implements the Helmholtz operator, constructed from the second-order wave equation including Stokes absorption.

    The Helmholtz operator acting on uuu is defined as

    (∇^2P−1ρ0∇ρ0⋅∇+2iω3α0c0+ω2c02)u \left(\hat \nabla^2 P - \frac{1}{\rho_0} \nabla \rho_0 \cdot \nabla + \frac{2i\omega^3\alpha_0}{c_0} + \frac{\omega^2}{c_0^2}\right) u (∇^2P−ρ0​1​∇ρ0​⋅∇+c0​2iω3α0​​+c02​ω2​)u

    where uuu is the pressure, ρ0\rho_0ρ0​ is the background density, α0\alpha_0α0​ is the background absorption, ω\omegaω is the angular frequency, and c0c_0c0​ is the speed of light.

    laplacian_with_pml

    Given a field uuu, a medium object medium and an angular frequency ω\omegaω, this operator computes the Laplacian of uuu modified with PML defined as in [Bermudez et al., 2007], where each partial derivative is modified as

    ∂2∂ε2→1γε∂∂ε1γε∂∂ε \frac{\partial^2}{\partial \varepsilon^2} \to \frac{1}{\gamma_\varepsilon}\frac{\partial}{\partial \varepsilon}\frac{1}{\gamma_\varepsilon}\frac{\partial}{\partial \varepsilon} ∂ε2∂2​→γε​1​∂ε∂​γε​1​∂ε∂​

    The operator is further augmented by a second term, which takes into account a potential heterogeneous ambient density ρ0\rho_0ρ0​, giving the final form of

    (∇^2P−1ρ0∇ρ0⋅∇)u \left(\hat \nabla^2 P - \frac{1}{\rho_0} \nabla \rho_0 \cdot \nabla\right) u (∇^2P−ρ0​1​∇ρ0​⋅∇)u

    where uuu is the pressure and ∇^\hat \nabla∇^ is the modified Laplacian.

    wavevector

    The wavevector operator acting on a field uuu is defined as

    (2iω3α0c0+ω2c02)u \left(\frac{2i\omega^3\alpha_0}{c_0} + \frac{\omega^2}{c_0^2}\right) u (c0​2iω3α0​​+c02​ω2​)u

    where α0\alpha_0α0​ is the absorption coefficient of the medium, c0c_0c0​ is the speed of sound in the medium, and ω\omegaω is the angular frequency. The absorption coefficient is defined in units of dB/(MHz^y cm).
